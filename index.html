Who I am

I love telling stories and building brand narratives—the kind that make you pause mid-scroll.

I'm a recent business graduate from Shaheed Sukhdev College of Business Studies, University of Delhi. I find myself constantly noticing the little things — why some campaigns stay with us, why some don't, and how the right words at the right moment can change everything.

I like my marketing thoughtful, a little playful, and rooted in insight. I want my work to feel human, a little unexpected, and maybe a little magical.

I'm currently working in social media. When I'm not thinking about content strategy, I'm writing on Medium — essays on brands, culture, and whatever's been sitting too long in my head.

How I think

I start with the feeling a brand wants to create, then work backwards to the words and formats that create it. I don't begin with a caption — I begin with a question: what does this person need to feel when they stop scrolling? Relieved? Understood? Like they're in on something? Once I know that, everything else — the tone, the hook, the CTA — follows pretty naturally. I'm drawn to brands that have something real to say and just need the right way to say it. That's the gap I try to live in.

कॉफ़ी और कहानी (KAK) — Indie Coffee Brand

A full business proposal for an indie café concept built around storytelling, Indian warmth, and specialty coffee. I developed the brand name, identity, positioning, competitive landscape, and four campaign ideas from scratch — all rooted in the idea that every cup becomes part of a story.

#1
Every Cup Has A Story — Story cards on every table. Best entries featured weekly. Turns the café experience into a living anthology.

#4
Monsoon Diaries — Rainy-day memories turned into illustrated Instagram posts. Shareable, seasonal, and very KAK.

Brand Analysis · Content Strategy · Consumer Psychology

Decoding Plum Goodness — An Independent Brand Analysis

A deep dive into Plum Goodness' brand positioning, content strategy, and where it can build stronger emotional connection. This wasn't a brief I was given — it was a question I couldn't stop turning over: how does a brand with this much warmth still feel slightly distant?

From Volume to Value — A Content Strategy Reset

Plum posts near-daily, creating content saturation. The recommendation: shift to 3–4 curated posts per week with a quality bar of "would someone screenshot this to send a friend?" If not, it shouldn't go up.

'Ingredient Diaries' — Founder-Led Trust Building

The founder is an IIT Bombay Chemical Engineering graduate — and this credential is nearly invisible in their content. A recurring founder-led series could build a category of trust that polished brand content simply can't.

'Brand Diaries' — Humanising the Brand

Show the people behind it. Younger team members, in their own voice. Not a corporate culture reel — genuinely unpolished content that makes Plum feel like a community, not a company.

'Product Personalities' — Making Skincare Entertaining

Plum is one of the few Indian skincare brands warm enough to pull off character-driven content. Competitors like Minimalist and mCaffeine lead with function. This lane is genuinely open.

Key Insight Plum has scientific credibility AND emotional warmth — rare in Indian D2C beauty
Market Context FY25: ₹402 Cr revenue, 23.3% YoY growth, first profitable year
Competitive Threat HUL acquired Minimalist for ₹2,955 Cr — Plum's window to own emotional positioning is now
